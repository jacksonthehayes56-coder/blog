Digital campaigns — for events, fundraisers, or product launches — can feel chaotic when everything lives in someone’s head or scattered notes.

A simple checklist can make your work more repeatable and less stressful, especially when you have rotating staff, volunteers, or student teams.

At [Volta NYC](https://voltanyc.org), student pods often create campaign checklists for the organizations they work with so future efforts are easier to run. Here’s how you can adopt the same approach.

## 1. Map your campaign stages

Most digital campaigns follow a similar arc:

- Planning
- Promotion
- Live period
- Follow-up

For each stage, list the tasks you typically handle. Don’t worry about order yet — just get everything out.

## 2. Turn tasks into a checklist

For example, for an event campaign:

**Planning**

- Define the goal (attendance, sign-ups, donations)
- Confirm date, time, location, and speakers
- Create a registration form or ticket link

**Promotion**

- Draft and schedule email announcements
- Create social posts (graphics + copy) for each platform
- Update the event on your website

**Live period**

- Post day-of reminders
- Share behind-the-scenes photos or clips

**Follow-up**

- Send thank-you email and post-event survey
- Share key highlights and outcomes online

## 3. Assign owners and deadlines

A checklist is more powerful when it’s clear who does what.

- Add a column for "Owner" next to each task
- Add another for "Due date" relative to the event or campaign start

This makes it easier to bring in student collaborators or new staff.

## 4. Improve the checklist over time

After each campaign, take 15 minutes to:

- Mark which tasks were unnecessary
- Add any missing steps
- Note what you’d change next time

Your checklist becomes a living document that captures institutional knowledge.
